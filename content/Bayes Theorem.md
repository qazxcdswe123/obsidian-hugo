$$P(H|E) =\dfrac {P(H) \cdot P(E|H)} {P(E)} $$

## When to Use
1. You have a hypothesis ${P(H) \cdot P(E|H)}$
2. You've observed some evidence ${P(E)}$
3. You want: $P(\dfrac{\text{Hypothesis}}{\text{given the evidence}})$ aka $P(H|E)$

## Proof
$P(H|E)$ = $P(E|H)$
